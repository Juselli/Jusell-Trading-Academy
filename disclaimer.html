1. Nature of Trading Risks

Trading in financial markets involves significant risks, including but not limited to:

  • Market Risk: Prices can move against your positions rapidly and unpredictably
  • Leverage Risk: Using leverage can amplify both profits and losses
  • Liquidity Risk: You may not be able to exit positions when desired
  • Volatility Risk: High volatility can result in substantial losses
  • Gap Risk: Markets can gap beyond your stop-loss levels
  • Systemic Risk: Broader market events can affect all positions

7. Market-Specific Risks

Futures Trading Risks:

  • High leverage can result in substantial losses
  • Margin calls may force position closures
  • Contract expiration dates must be managed
  • Overnight gaps can exceed stop-loss levels

Forex Trading Risks:

  • Currency values can fluctuate rapidly
  • Political and economic events affect exchange rates
  • High leverage amplifies both gains and losses
  • Weekend gaps can occur when markets reopen

Stock Trading Risks:

  • Individual company performance affects stock prices
  • Market sentiment can drive irrational price movements
  • Earnings announcements can cause significant volatility
  • Sector-specific risks can impact multiple holdings

Cryptocurrency Trading Risks:

  • Extreme volatility and price swings
  • Regulatory uncertainty and potential bans
  • Technology risks and security breaches
  • Limited liquidity in some markets

8. Technology and Platform Risks

Trading involves technology risks including:
